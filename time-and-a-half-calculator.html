What is Time and a Half?

Time and a half means earning 1.5× your regular hourly rate for overtime hours. It is the minimum overtime rate required by the US Fair Labor Standards Act (FLSA) for non-exempt employees who work more than 40 hours in a week. If you earn $20/hr normally, your time-and-a-half rate is $30/hr.

Frequently Asked Questions

What does time and a half mean? +
Time and a half means your pay rate is multiplied by 1.5. For every hour of overtime, you earn your normal hourly wage plus half of it again. Example: $20/hr × 1.5 = $30/hr overtime rate.
When is time and a half required by law? +
In the US, the FLSA requires time and a half for non-exempt employees working more than 40 hours per week. Some states (like California) also require it for hours over 8 in a single workday.
Is time and a half required on weekends or holidays? +
Federal law does not require overtime pay specifically for weekends or holidays — it is based on total hours worked per week. However, many employers voluntarily pay time and a half or double time on holidays.
What is the difference between time and a half and double time? +
Time and a half = 1.5× your regular rate. Double time = 2× your regular rate. Double time is typically paid for extreme overtime (e.g., over 12 hrs/day in California) or holidays, depending on your employer's policy or state law.
