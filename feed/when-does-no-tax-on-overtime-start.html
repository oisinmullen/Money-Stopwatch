When Does No Tax on Overtime Start?

The no-tax-on-overtime deduction is effective for tax year 2025 — meaning overtime earned on or after January 1, 2025 qualifies. Here is the complete legislative timeline and what workers should expect going forward.

Short Answer

The no-tax-on-overtime deduction started January 1, 2025 (retroactively applied when the law was signed in July 2025). Workers will claim the deduction on their 2025 federal tax return filed in early 2026.

Legislative Timeline

2025 Q1–Q2
Congressional debate and bill advancement. The One Big Beautiful Bill Act — a broad reconciliation package — worked through the House and Senate, with the no-tax-on-overtime provision as a key component aimed at working-class Americans who earn overtime.
July 2025
Signed into law. The One Big Beautiful Bill Act was signed, with the no-tax-on-overtime deduction retroactively effective for all of tax year 2025 (January 1, 2025 onward).
Late 2025
IRS guidance issued. The IRS began publishing updated withholding guidance to help employers adjust paycheck withholding to reflect the deduction for the remainder of 2025.
Jan–Apr 2026
Workers claim the deduction on 2025 tax returns. Qualifying employees will deduct eligible overtime wages when filing their 2025 federal income tax return. Even if your employer didn't adjust withholding in 2025, you can still claim the full deduction on your return.
2026 onward
Ongoing deduction. The deduction continues for tax years after 2025, with employers expected to incorporate it into regular payroll withholding so workers see the benefit in each paycheck rather than waiting until tax filing.

When Will I See It in My Paycheck?

For most workers, the first time you see the benefit is when you file your 2025 tax return in early 2026 — you will owe less federal income tax (or receive a larger refund) because of the overtime deduction.

Going forward into 2026 and beyond, employers who have updated their payroll systems to reflect IRS withholding guidance will withhold less federal income tax on overtime portions of your paycheck, so you see the extra money in real time rather than waiting for a refund.

If your employer has not yet updated withholding, you can still benefit at tax time. Consider adjusting your W-4 withholding allowances to account for the expected deduction so you are not over-withholding throughout the year.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does it apply to overtime I worked before the law was signed? +
Yes. The deduction is retroactive to January 1, 2025. Any qualifying overtime earned in 2025, even before the bill was signed in July, is eligible for the deduction on your 2025 tax return.
Do I need to do anything special when I file my 2025 taxes? +
You will need to report your overtime wages and claim the deduction on your federal return. Tax software and preparers will include a line or worksheet for the deduction once the IRS finalizes the 2025 form updates. Keep records of your overtime pay from your W-2 or pay stubs.
Will my paycheck automatically change? +
It depends on whether your employer has updated their payroll system to reflect the new withholding tables. If not, you still get the full benefit when you file your taxes. You can also submit an updated W-4 to your employer to reduce withholding throughout the year.
